## I – You aren't where you want to be because you aren't the person who would be there

When it comes to setting big goals, people tend to focus on one of the two requirements for success:
- Changing your actions to make progress toward the goal (least important, second order)
- Changing who you are so that your behavior naturally follows (most important, first order)

Most people set a surface-level goal, hype themselves up to remain disciplined for the first few weeks, then go back to their old ways without much struggle, because they were trying to build a great life on a rotting foundation.

If you want a specific outcome in life, you must have the lifestyle that creates that outcome long before you reach it.

## II – You aren't where you want to be because you don't want to be there

All behavior is goal-oriented (teleological). Most of the time, your goals are unconscious. You pursue goals that can harm you, but you justify your actions in a way that is socially acceptable.

Real change requires changing your goals — not surface-level goals, but changing your point of view. A goal is a projection into the future that acts as a lens of perception.

## III – You aren't where you want to be because you're afraid to be there

The anatomy of identity:
1. You want to achieve a goal
2. You perceive reality through the lens of that goal
3. You only notice "important" information
4. You act and receive feedback
5. You repeat until automatic (conditioning)
6. That behavior becomes part of who you think you are
7. You defend your identity to maintain psychological consistency
8. Your identity shapes new goals, restarting the cycle

When your identity feels threatened, you go into fight or flight — same as when your body feels threatened.

## IV – The life you want lies within a specific level of mind

9 stages of ego development: Impulsive → Self-Protective → Conformist → Self-Aware → Conscientious → Individualist → Strategist → Construct-Aware → Unitive

## V – Intelligence is the ability to get what you want out of life

Intelligence = agency + opportunity + the ability to iterate, persist, and understand the big picture (cybernetics).

High intelligence is realizing any problem can be solved on a large enough timescale.

## VI – How to launch into a completely new life (in 1 day)

Three phases: Dissonance → Uncertainty → Discovery

### Part 1: Morning – Psychological Excavation
Vision & Anti-Vision questions to uncover hidden motives.

### Part 2: Throughout The Day – Interrupting Autopilot
Scheduled reminders to break unconscious patterns.

### Part 3: Evening – Synthesizing Insight
Compress insights into anti-vision, vision MVP, and goals at three time horizons.

## VII – Turn Your Life Into A Video Game

6 components:
- Anti-vision: What you never want to experience again
- Vision: The ideal life you're building toward
- 1-year goal: The mission
- 1-month project: The boss fight
- Daily levers: The quests
- Constraints: The rules
